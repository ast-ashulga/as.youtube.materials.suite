## Summary

Fireship surveys 10 major tech trends for 2026. On AI: LLMs have plateaued — GPT-5 was a disappointment and the expected exponential capability gains are not showing up. AI coding tools are not replacing human engineers in 2026; they have instead created a new category of "code janitor" jobs for reviewing and cleaning the AI-generated code piling up in repositories. The Bureau of Labor Statistics still forecasts 15% software engineering job growth through 2034. Other highlights: humanoid robots entering mass production and a wave of major AI company IPOs (OpenAI, Anthropic) expected in 2026, which Fireship reads as a possible signal the hype cycle is peaking.

## Key Takeaways

- LLMs have plateaued: GPT-5 was a disappointment; the exponential intelligence leaps that were expected are not materializing
- AI coding tools are not replacing human engineers in 2026 — instead spawning "code janitor" roles focused on reviewing and fixing AI-generated code
- The developer job market has not recovered to 2023 levels, but the BLS still forecasts 15% software engineering job growth through 2034
- A wave of major AI company IPOs (OpenAI, Anthropic, SpaceX) is expected in 2026
- Quantum computing achieved the first-ever verifiable real-world algorithm outperforming supercomputers (Google quantum echoes)

## Notable Quotes

> "Despite what the optimist will tell you, AI coding tools are still nowhere close to replacing human engineers."
