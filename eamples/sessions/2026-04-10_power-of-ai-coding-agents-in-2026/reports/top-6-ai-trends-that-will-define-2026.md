## Summary

Jeff Su covers six data-backed AI trends for 2026 drawn from McKinsey, OpenAI, Stanford, and Epoch AI. The biggest structural shift is model commoditization: performance gaps between leading models are shrinking, shifting competition to the application layer. Contrary to the hype, 2026 is "the year of AI workflows, not AI agents" — fewer than 10% of organizations have scaled true autonomous agents, while McKinsey projects structured AI workflows could unlock ~$3 trillion by 2030. Non-technical workers are closing the gap with technical specialists: coding-related AI usage grew 36% in six months per OpenAI enterprise data. The most actionable point: context beats prompting — the key constraint is what information AI has access to, not how precisely requests are phrased.

## Key Takeaways

- AI model performance is converging — the "best model" debate matters less; Nvidia chips use 105,000x less energy per token than 10 years ago
- AI workflows — structured, human-supervised — are delivering more enterprise value than autonomous agents; McKinsey projects ~$3T unlocked by 2030
- True autonomous agents face significant hurdles; Andrej Karpathy: "this is the decade of agents, not a single breakthrough year"
- Non-technical employees used AI for coding-related tasks 36% more in just 6 months (OpenAI enterprise report)
- Context beats prompting: the key constraint is what information AI has access to, not how precisely you phrase requests
- Platform lock-in is accelerating — whoever holds your context (emails, docs, calendar) holds your AI advantage
- The jagged frontier means expertise is being reset — those who learn faster gain compounding competitive advantage

## Action Items

- [ ] Stop comparing AI models on benchmark scores; evaluate based on integration with your actual tools and workflows
- [ ] Turn your most-used successful prompts into repeatable documented workflows
- [ ] Attempt one "impossible task" this month using AI (dashboard, dataset, automation) to calibrate capabilities
- [ ] Audit and consolidate where your information lives — scattered files prevent AI from helping effectively
- [ ] Start learning now rather than waiting — the jagged frontier reset means early movers compound advantage

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| AI Workflows | Structured, repeatable processes integrating AI with humans in the loop — currently delivering more enterprise value than autonomous agents |
| Model Commoditization | Performance gaps between AI models shrinking, shifting competitive advantage to the application and integration layer |
| The Fact Gap | AI models know the public internet but lack private organizational context — the key constraint |
| Jagged Frontier | Ethan Mollick's concept: AI capabilities are uneven and rapidly shifting, creating windows for faster learners to gain advantage |
| Context (in AI) | Private information (files, docs, emails, calendars) provided to AI — increasingly more important than prompt wording |
